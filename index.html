Surveillance, Analysis & Bioinformatics

Approach

My approach blends routine surveillance, laboratory confirmation, and sequence-based analysis where available. I use structured epidemiological data to support detection of trends and genomic tools to investigate pathogen evolution and spread.

Example: Dengue Fever

Dengue is a mosquito-borne viral disease caused by four serotypes. Combining laboratory diagnostics with genomic surveillance and geospatial case data improves detection of emerging strains and informs targeted control measures.

Tools & Outputs

  • Case reporting systems & outbreak line lists
  • Sequence alignment and phylogenetic inference (bioinformatics workflows)
  • Visualizable outputs for public health teams and policy makers

Public Education & Communication

Communication approach

I prioritize clear, evidence-based messaging tailored to the audience—whether community members, health workers, or policy makers—to improve understanding, reduce stigma, and promote preventive behaviors.

Examples

  • Short community leaflets explaining dengue prevention and warning signs
  • Infographics summarizing surveillance findings for local health teams
  • Workshops on sample collection and data reporting for field staff
